Food portion: Nutrition: The Fuel Behind My Transformation from 140kg to 75kg

  • Travel, Lifestyle
  • 03 Comments

Diving deeper into my fitness journey, I found that the landscape was more complex than I initially thought. Exercise was a crucial component, no doubt, but there was an equally important facet that I had yet to explore fully ‚Äì nutrition. The more I understood its impact, the more I realized it was not just a supporting actor, but a star that held its own on the stage of my transformation.

The task was enormous ‚Äì untangling the intricate web of nutrition science, understanding the relationship between our bodies and the food we consume. I immersed myself in learning about macronutrients: proteins, carbohydrates, and fats. I delved into the mystery of how these elements interacted within my body and how to strike a balance in my diet that would fuel my workouts and foster my weight loss.

Armed with this newfound knowledge, I began my most challenging mission yet ‚Äì overhauling my eating habits. I took to the kitchen like a warrior preparing for battle, my weapons being wholesome ingredients, a meal plan, and an unwavering determination. I cooked my meals, experimented with nutritious recipes, swapped refined and processed foods with natural, unprocessed ones. I found joy in creating colorful, balanced meals that not only looked good but made my body feel good too.

It wasn't an overnight change. I started by reducing my meal portions. Where I once heaped my plate full, now I was more conscious, more mindful. From four plates, I gradually brought it down to one, ensuring it was balanced with proteins, carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

The fight against junk food was one of the hardest battles I fought. The temptation was strong, but my resolve was stronger. I bid farewell to chips, fast food, and sweets, keeping them out of my reach for six solid months at the outset. The absence of their salty, greasy, sugary taste was strange at first, but with time I came to appreciate the natural flavors of my healthy meals.

I also quit soft drinks, letting go of the carbonated sweetness that was a staple in my previous diet. In its place, I welcomed natural alternatives like coconut water, fresh juices, and plenty of water. It was a refreshing change, quite literally. My body reacted positively, and I felt more hydrated, more energetic.

This monumental shift in my dietary habits wasn't easy. There were days of struggle, moments of weakness, but I persevered. As my diet changed, so did my body. I could feel a surge in my energy levels, an improvement in my workouts. But the most significant change was in my physical appearance. The mirror reflected a healthier, happier version of me. The scale, once a symbol of fear, now indicated my victory ‚Äì I had gone from 140kg to 75kg in two years.

The journey was a roller coaster ride of emotions, challenges, and victories. It wasn't easy, but the person I've become today ‚Äì healthier, stronger, and confident, tells me it was worth every bit of effort. I stand as living proof that change is possible, that anyone can turn their life around. If I could do it, so can anyone else who dares to try.

Vatsal Shah
